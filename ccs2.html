CRYPTOGRAPHY AND CYBER SECURITY



Cryptography is the practice and study of techniques used to secure communication and information from unauthorized access or modification. It involves the use of mathematical algorithms to convert plain text into unreadable ciphertext and vice versa, ensuring that only authorized parties can access the original information.

Cybersecurity, on the other hand, refers to the protection of computer systems, networks, and data from potential threats and attacks. It encompasses a wide range of practices, technologies, and strategies aimed at safeguarding digital assets and preventing unauthorized access, data breaches, malware infections, and other cyber threats.

OBJECTIVES :

  • Confidentiality:

    • To protect sensitive information from unauthorized access or disclosure by using encryption techniques to render the data unreadable without the appropriate decryption key.

  • Integrity:

    • Ensuring that data remains unaltered and trustworthy during storage, transmission, and processing. Cryptographic hash functions and digital signatures are used to detect any unauthorized modifications.

  • Authentication:

    • Verifying the identity of users, systems, or devices to prevent unauthorized access. Techniques like passwords, multi-factor authentication, and digital certificates are employed.

  • Non-Repudiation:

    • Ensuring that the sender of a message cannot deny sending it, and the receiver cannot deny receiving it. Digital signatures play a crucial role in providing non-repudiation.

  • Availability:

    • Ensuring that systems and data are accessible and operational when needed, guarding against denial-of-service (DoS) attacks and other disruptions.

  • Privacy:

    • Protecting individuals' personal information and data from being accessed or misused by unauthorized entities.

  • Data Integrity:

    • Verifying that data has not been corrupted or tampered with during storage or transmission, which is vital for maintaining the trustworthiness of information.

  • Risk Management:

    • Identifying potential security risks, assessing their impact, and implementing measures to mitigate those risks effectively.

  • Incident Response:

    • Having a well-defined plan to detect, respond, and recover from cybersecurity incidents, such as data breaches or cyberattacks.

  • Regulatory Compliance:

    • Ensuring adherence to relevant laws, regulations, and industry standards related to data protection and cybersecurity.
